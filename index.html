Case Study

The business story behind the data — situation, approach, and measurable impact

1

🏢 Situation

Pangkalan Gas Joeherman is a subsidized LPG 3kg distribution point in Pontianak, serving both Household (RT) and SME (UMKM) segments. After establishing the 2023 baseline, the owner needed to understand whether the business was growing and how government policy changes were affecting the customer mix.

2

⚠️ Challenge

Key questions that needed answering:

  • How much did the business actually grow year-over-year?
  • What was the real impact of the government's household-priority policy on UMKM customers?
  • Were seasonal patterns consistent or shifting?
  • Should the distribution strategy change based on new data?
3

🔍 Approach

Performed comprehensive YoY comparison across 69,182 combined transactions (2023: 31,459 + 2024: 37,723):

  • Transaction volume & profit trend — monthly, quarterly, annual
  • Segment shift analysis — RT vs UMKM share migration tracking
  • Policy impact quantification — measured government regulation effects
  • Seasonal pattern confirmation — verified September peak & December trough
  • Growth decomposition — organic growth vs policy-driven shift
4

📊 Key Findings

Total transactions grew +19.9% (6,264 more) and annual profit surged from Rp 94.3M → Rp 113.2M (+18.9%). Household share rose from 82.1% → 83.4% due to policy. However, UMKM grew in absolute terms (+631 txn, +11.2%) despite a smaller share — indicating an expanding total market. September remained peak month, up 42.8% YoY.

5

🚀 Recommendations Delivered

  • Adaptive policy strategy — balance RT compliance with UMKM retention
  • Q3 buffer stock plan — pre-September inventory buildup based on proven pattern
  • UMKM growth capture — targeted programs for the organically growing segment
  • Q4 demand smoothing — address consistent December-January dips

Case Study

The business story behind the data — situation, approach, and measurable impact

1

🏢 Situation

PT DKCMI is the KOBELCO heavy machinery dealer in West Kalimantan, serving mining and construction companies. The spare parts division handles 319 unique parts across 13 material groups, with total sales of Rp 17.6 Billion over 9 months — but inventory decisions were largely manual.

2

⚠️ Challenge

The warehouse faced critical issues:

  • Recurring stock-outs on fast-moving items, causing machine downtime for customers
  • Over-stocking of slow-moving parts, tying up capital
  • Extreme customer concentration — 95.7% revenue from essentially 2 customers
  • No data-driven reorder points or safety stock calculations
3

🔍 Approach

Analyzed 7,153 transactions across the full Jan-Sep 2023 period:

  • Sales trend analysis — monthly patterns tied to heavy machinery maintenance cycles
  • Customer concentration risk assessment — Pareto analysis of revenue dependency
  • Fast-moving item identification — ranked 319 parts by velocity and value
  • Material group performance matrix — 13 groups evaluated on margin & volume
  • Predictive reorder point modeling based on consumption rates
4

📊 Key Findings

OIL HYD 68LL dominates at Rp 1.29B (2,320 units) — must never stock out. Top 5 parts = 20.6% of total revenue. March peak (Rp 1.40B) correlates with Q1 maintenance season. PT Kalimantan Inti Maju contributes 95.7% of revenue — a significant concentration risk requiring diversification.

5

🚀 Recommendations Delivered

  • Safety stock framework — minimum stock levels for top 20 fast-moving parts
  • Predictive reorder model — consumption-based triggers to prevent stock-outs
  • Customer diversification strategy — targeting other mining companies in West Kalimantan
  • Margin optimization playbook — upselling high-margin groups (Undercarriage, Filters)

Case Study

The business story behind the data — situation, approach, and measurable impact

1

🏢 Situation

PT JAR Andalan Rasa operates a Japanese restaurant in Pontianak with 60+ menu items across 5 categories. Revenue was concentrated on dine-in (92.5%) with minimal online channel utilization, despite the growing food delivery market in Indonesia.

2

⚠️ Challenge

Management had no data-driven visibility into:

  • Which menu items actually drive profit vs. just volume
  • Optimal staffing during peak vs. off-peak hours
  • Why online channels (GoFood/GrabFood) represented only 2.8% of revenue
  • Whether the heavy reliance on one hero product (Kare Ramen) was a risk
3

🔍 Approach

Conducted full EDA on 7,437 records covering 2,254 transactions (Rp 164.8M net sales):

  • Menu performance analysis — identified hero products & underperformers
  • Peak hour mapping — pinpointed the 17:00-19:00 "golden hours" contributing 43% revenue
  • Sales channel decomposition — quantified the dine-in vs online gap
  • Payment behavior analysis — discovered near-50/50 cash vs digital split
  • Daily & weekly trend analysis to optimize scheduling
4

📊 Key Findings

Kare Ramen alone generates Rp 23.7M (14.4% of total revenue) — nearly 2× the #2 item. Online channels at just 2.8% represent an estimated Rp 30-50M untapped annual opportunity. Saturday peaks at Rp 31.6M, while Monday drops to Rp 18.2M — a 73% variance that can be smoothed with targeted promotions.

5

🚀 Recommendations Delivered

  • Menu diversification strategy — reduce Kare Ramen dependency, promote tier-2 items
  • Online channel activation plan — GoFood/GrabFood promo during off-peak hours
  • Staff optimization schedule — concentrate resources during golden hours (17-19)
  • Weekday promotion framework — address Monday-Tuesday revenue dip
